About Kelpie Web Services

From Penfolds to pixels

I started my career as a cellar hand and winemaker at Penfolds, crafting wine with precision and care. These days, I craft websites with the same attention to detail, but the mission is different: making the digital world accessible and straightforward for everyone who needs it.

I hold a Graduate Diploma in Computer Science and I'm currently completing my Masters. I've worked as a UX professional and frontend developer, building websites for both small businesses and large companies. Along the way, I completed a web development bootcamp and learned that the best websites aren't just technically sound—they're built with real people in mind.

Why accessibility matters

I've worked with and around disability from a young age, and I've seen firsthand how easy it is for organisations to forget entire groups of people when designing digital experiences. Accessibility isn't a checkbox to me. It's about respect, inclusion, and making sure no one gets left behind.

That's why every website I build meets WCAG 2.2 Level AA standards. Not because it's required (though it is now, as of January 2025), but because it's the right thing to do.

Meet Muffin

Kelpie Web Services is named after Muffin, my 18-month-old working line Kelpie from New South Wales. She's fiercely intelligent, always ready for work, and aims to please. She loves deeply, works tirelessly, and is obedient and respectful. She's currently laying at my feet as I write this.

Muffin's the perfect mascot for this business because she embodies the traits I bring to every project: intelligence, attentiveness, loyalty, and drive. She's a working dog, and this is a working partnership.

Why I started this

Honestly? I started Kelpie Web Services because I kept seeing government agencies paying $40-80K and waiting 6-12 months for websites that should cost half as much and take a quarter of the time.

It felt predatory. Taxpayer money going to bloated agencies that deliver slowly, communicate poorly, and treat accessibility like an afterthought.

I knew I could do better. Build faster, communicate clearly, charge fairly, and actually deliver websites that work for everyone who needs them. So that's what I'm doing.

My values

Accessibility

Every website I build is WCAG 2.2 Level AA compliant. I maintain a checklist of disabilities and accessibility considerations to make sure I'm designing for everyone, not just the majority.

Transparency

You'll always know where your project stands, what you're paying for, and when you'll launch. No surprises, no hidden fees, no corporate runarounds.

Reliability

I deliver on time and on budget. If something changes, you'll know immediately. I respect your time and your schedule.

Based in Adelaide, serving South Australia

I work from home in Adelaide and happily serve clients across South Australia, including regional and rural areas. For now, I'm keeping things local—but if you're outside SA and we're a good fit, let's talk.

Looking ahead

In the future, I'd love to build a team that reflects the inclusive values I design for. Teaching web development to people with disabilities and creating meaningful employment opportunities would be the ultimate goal. But first things first: let's build some great websites.
